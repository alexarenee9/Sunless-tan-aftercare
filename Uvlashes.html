Wait, UV lashes?

Instead of traditional adhesive that air-dries and makes you hold your breath, I use a UV light-cured adhesive that bonds each lash instantly with a quick flash of light. Faster, stronger, and way more comfortable. No fumes, no stickies, no drama. Just really good lashes. 💜

🔬 yes, it's totally safe

The light used has been independently lab tested and certified to international safety standards. It emits light that is 400 times gentler than the sun and even 60 times dimmer than what's considered a safe limit. It does not penetrate skin tissue and is actually safer than traditional lash glue, which contains more harsh chemicals. You're in good hands!

FAQ
QDoes it hurt?
Nope. Most clients feel absolutely nothing. If you've been dreading this part, you can officially stop. It's genuinely a non-event.
QHow long does it take?
A full set typically takes 2.5 to 3 hours depending on your lash style and natural lashes. Refreshes are 1.5 or 2 hours since I do a lash bath before every appointment. Bring a podcast or just enjoy the nap!
QI have sensitive eyes. Can I still get lashes?
This system is honestly a game changer for sensitive clients. The adhesive has significantly less of the irritating chemicals found in traditional adhesive, and the instant curing means almost no fumes. Many clients who swore they'd "tried lashes and it didn't work" have had zero issues with this method. And full transparency, I personally cannot wear lashes done with traditional adhesive. This is the only system I can use on myself. Tested, approved, and worn by yours truly. 💜 Always mention your sensitivities when you book so we can plan accordingly!
QWill they damage my natural lashes?
When applied correctly, not a chance. I actually care about the health of your natural lashes and will never put on a weight or length that's going to cause damage. Your naturals stay safe, I promise.
QCan I wear mascara?
You technically can on the tips only, but I promise you won't want to. Once you see these lashes you're going to retire your mascara wand for good. If you must, water-based and oil-free only, and never waterproof, it's a retention killer.
QWhen should I come back for a fill?
Most clients come back every 2 to 3 weeks for a refresh and can stretch to 4 weeks if life gets in the way. Past 4 weeks though? We're probably starting over. Book before you're sad about it. 😂

Lash Aftercare

✓ yes, do these things

  • ✓Clean your lashes every day with a lash-safe cleanser. Clean lashes last longer!
  • ✓Brush them daily with a clean spoolie after they're fully dry, especially after waking up
  • ✓Sleep on a silk pillowcase. Your skin will love it too. You're welcome.
  • ✓Use oil-free skincare and makeup around the eye area. Oil is not your lashes' friend.
  • ✓Book your refresh every 2 to 3 weeks to keep them looking full!

✕ please just don't

  • ✕Picking or pulling at your lashes. Your natural ones come with them and I will cry... and so will you because it hurts!
  • ✕Heavy oil-based makeup removers or eye creams right on the lash line. This system is way more forgiving than old school lashes but oil can still break down the bond over time.
  • ✕Waterproof mascara. It requires oil to remove and will take your lashes with it.
  • ✕Rubbing your eyes. We know it feels good. We know. Just don't. 😭
  • ✕Steam rooms or saunas frequently, occasional is okay but daily is not.
